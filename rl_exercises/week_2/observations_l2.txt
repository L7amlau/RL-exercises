Week 2 - Level 2: Decreasing Information Flow

Question
What happens if only step() is available (no explicit dynamics T and no reward function R)? Do Policy Iteration and Value Iteration still work?

Short answer
Policy Iteration and Value Iteration are model-based methods. They explicitly require T(s,a,s') and R(s,a).
With only step() access, they do not work directly, but only indirectly via an estimated model (model learning).

Observations from the runs
1) Deterministic MarsRover with Policy Iteration
- Converged policy: [1 1 1 1 1]
- Final eval reward: 90.0 (with 3 eval episodes)
- Interpretation: With a complete model, very good policies are found quickly.

2) Stochastic MarsRover (0.5/0.5) with Value Iteration
- Example policy (seed 0): [1 1 1 0 0]
- Final eval reward: 3.0 (with 3 episodes), much more stable with 30 episodes
- With 30 episodes and gamma=0.5: Final eval reward 25.5
- With 30 episodes and gamma=0.99: Final eval reward in the range of about 18.3 to 25.4 (seeds 0,1,2)
- Interpretation: In the stochastic setting and with few eval episodes, the estimate is highly noisy.

Answer to the guiding question
- Without model access, Policy Iteration and Value Iteration do not apply directly.
- With only step(), T_hat and R_hat must first be estimated from data.
- Then PI/VI can be applied to the estimated model (approximate planning).

Conclusion
The less information flow is available (samples instead of an exact model), the worse/more unstable classical planning becomes, and the more important data-driven or model-free methods become.
