Week 3 - Level 2 Observations: SARSA vs. Q-Learning on MarsRover

Setup
- training_steps=20000, eval_every_n_steps=2000, n_eval_episodes=30
- alpha=0.1, gamma=0.99, epsilon=0.1
- seeds: 0, 1, 2

Results (final eval reward)
- SARSA: 9.0, 90.0, 50.0 (mean 49.67)
- Q-Learning: 90.0, 90.0, 9.0 (mean 63.00)

Observations
1) Both methods can learn high-return behavior, but both are highly seed-sensitive.
2) Q-Learning performed better on average in this setup.
3) SARSA was often more conservative and showed slower/less stable improvement in some seeds.

Interpretation
- SARSA is on-policy (update uses the actually chosen next action).
- Q-Learning is off-policy (update uses max over next-state actions), which can speed up value propagation.
- Under epsilon-greedy exploration and sparse useful trajectories, high variance across seeds is expected.

Conclusion
Q-Learning is stronger on average here, but neither method is fully stable. Multi-seed evaluation is necessary for reliable conclusions.
